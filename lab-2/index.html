Lamborghini: An Approach to the Organization Study

Abstract

We know that Lamborghini is a very expensive brand and is not accessible to everyone and this makes it interesting to investigate to whom it is accessible...

Keywords

Lamborghini; SWOT; Strategy; Management

1 – The Company

Lamborghini is a car manufacturer founded by Ferruccio Lamborghini in 1963. Now, the company is owned by the Volkswagen Group. Automobili Lamborghini’s headquarter is in Sant’Agata Bolognese, in Northern Italy. (Lamborghini, 2022) During the 2000s, the company introduced a new brand’s portfolio with new products and model lines.

The cars are known to be stylish, to have high performance capacity and to be very expensive. They created famous cars. Indeed, the company currently produces two super sport car models the V12-powered Aventador, launched in 2011, and the V10- powered Huracán, launched in 2014, along with the Urus Super SUV powered by a twin-turbo V8 engine, launched in 2017. (Lamborghini, 2022)

2 – SWOT Analysis

Strengths

  • World Renowned Name: Almost everyone can recognize the name Lamborghini.
  • Technology: Belonging to Volkswagen group, Lamborghini has access to state-of-the-art tech.

Weaknesses

  • High Price: The high price faces the most backlash at times of economic crises.
  • High Fuel Consumption: The powerful cars use loads of fuel, threatening the environment.

Opportunities

  • Expand the Availability: Right now, Lamborghini is out of reach for many people.
  • Increase in Better Customer Service It’s a hard to source parts of a Lamborghini model if something goes wrong.

Threats

  • Competitors: Competitors are rising, producing ultra-premium cars.
  • Government policies: Government policies against high fuel consuming super cars.

3 – Current Strategy

Lamborghini has performed well in recent years and expectations for the future are high. Lamborghini has survived the pandemic and continued to grow thanks to its resilience.

Lamborghini's sales, turnover, operating results, and profitability are at an all-time high. Continued success and a positive market response reinforce the company's stability and flexibility in the face of uncertainty and challenges and despite the ongoing pandemic.

4 – Leadership

The Chairman and Chief Executive Officer of Lamborghini is Stephan Winkelmann. He was born on the 18th of october in Berlin. He grew up in Rome. Stephan Winkelmann studied political science in Rome and received his degree in Munich, Germany. Before moving to the automotive industry, he did a professional career at a German financial institution.

The first company he worked for was Mercedes-Benz followed by that he started for Fiat Auto from 1994 to 2004. He worked there until he was appointed as the CEO of Fiat Auto Austria, Switzerland and Germany.

5 – Conclusion

We selected Lamborghini because we found it intriguing to see how Lamborghini markets its goods to a certain target market. Who do they sell these pricey products to and how do they do it? We didn’t know a lot about the Lamborghini, their background, organizational structure, and management style.

We found out the Lamborghini, provide a SWOT analysis, and discussed their current leadership structure and business strategy.
